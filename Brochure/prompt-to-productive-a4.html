About the Program

Prompt to Productive is a four day AI Fluency program designed for university students who want to move past casual chatbot use and into deliberate, skillful collaboration with artificial intelligence. Across four guided sessions of roughly ninety minutes each, participants build the mental models, prompt writing discipline, ethical judgment, and platform awareness that separate a confused user from a confident operator.

Each day pairs short concept blocks with hands on practice, so students leave the room with prompts, templates, and habits they can apply the very next morning to coursework, projects, internships, and applications. By the final session, every participant will have shaped a personal AI workflow, a private learning context document, a study system, a career toolkit, and a written collaboration policy that reflects their own values.

Day 01
Session One  ·  90 Minutes
01

The Hidden Mechanics of AI

Foundations of how modern AI actually works, and how to think with it instead of around it. By the end of the session, participants will hold a clear mental model of the machine behind the chat box, a working framework for fluency, and a personal code for ethical use.

01

A Way of Thinking About AI That Stays With You

A clear mental model of what large language models are, what they can and cannot do, and why understanding the machine behind the chat box quietly changes every prompt you ever write.

02

The Frameworks of AI Fluency

A four part fluency model covering Delegation, Description, Discernment, and Diligence that turns scattered chatbot use into a repeatable workflow you can teach a friend in one sitting.

03

Prompt Fundamentals

The anatomy of a strong prompt, the role of context, instruction, examples, and constraints, and the small phrasing choices that quietly double the quality of an answer.

04

Diligence and Ethics

A working definition of responsible use, how to spot hallucinations, when to disclose AI assistance, and how to keep your academic integrity firmly intact across every assignment.

05

Building a Personal Learning Context Document

A single living file that captures who you are, what you study, how you write, and how you think, so every future chat begins with you already understood.

06

Fueling Your AI Assistant

The practical craft of feeding the model the right inputs, in the right order, at the right depth, rather than typing one short line and hoping the output happens to land.

07

Automation versus Augmentation

A clear line between tasks worth handing over entirely and tasks where AI should sit beside you rather than in front of you, with examples drawn from real student workloads.

08

Being the Human in the Loop

What it actually means to stay in charge of judgment, decisions, and final outputs when an AI is doing most of the typing, and the checkpoints that keep your work honestly yours.

09

Responsible Use in Academic and Professional Life

A personal code of conduct for classrooms, assignments, group projects, internships, and the working years that follow, written in your own words rather than borrowed policy.

10

Session Practical: Your First Production Prompt

A guided exercise where each participant rewrites a real coursework prompt using the frameworks above, then compares before and after results to feel the difference firsthand.

Day 02
Session Two  ·  90 Minutes
02

AI as Your Study Partner

Turning the chat window from a quick answer machine into a long term tutor, a study coach, and a private learning archive. Participants leave with a tutor configuration, a fact check routine, study asset workflows, and a journaling habit they can carry across semesters.

01

Partner Versus Answer Engine

The mindset shift from asking AI to finish your thinking to inviting it to think alongside you, and why the second mode produces graduates who actually understand their own degree.

02

Configuring AI as Your Tutor

Setting up custom instructions, system prompts, and personas that make the model behave like a patient subject expert rather than a generic assistant chasing the next reply.

03

A Fallback System for Returning to a Semester

Building a structured set of summaries, flashcards, and recap prompts so that opening a new term feels like resuming a saved game rather than starting your studies from scratch.

04

Fact Checking Whether You Actually Learned

Simple Socratic prompt patterns the model can run on you, including blind recall checks, concept transfer questions, and explain it back routines that expose shaky understanding.

05

Approaches to Building Your Study Assets

Practical workflows for turning lectures, slides, and textbooks into clean notes, mind maps, practice problems, mock quizzes, and exam ready summaries you can revisit in minutes.

06

The Living Learning Journal

A weekly journaling practice, supported by reusable prompt templates, that tracks what you studied, what you understood, what is still fuzzy, and how your thinking is changing over months.

07

Memory and Continuity Across Sessions

How to use saved instructions, project folders, and reusable context blocks so that your tutor remembers you and your subjects, even when the chat window itself forgets.

08

Designing a Personal Curriculum

Using AI to convert a vague goal such as getting better at statistics into a week by week study plan with checkpoints, review cycles, and an honest weekly self assessment.

09

Note Compression and Active Recall Prompts

Ready to copy prompts that shrink long readings into layered notes, then quiz you on those notes without giving away the answers, so revision becomes practice instead of rereading.

10

Session Practical: Build Your Subject Companion

Each participant configures a tutor for one current course, generates a study pack, and runs a self test, leaving the room with a working assistant for an actual subject they study.

Day 03
Session Three  ·  90 Minutes
03

Career Mastery With AI

A grounded approach to using AI across career exploration, skill building, the job hunt, applications, and interviews. The focus is on raising your standards without losing your own voice, so the materials you send out still sound like a real person worth meeting.

01

Career Exploration Without Guesswork

Structured prompts for mapping your skills, interests, and constraints against real roles, industries, and career paths, so direction becomes a research process rather than a hope.

02

Skill Building With Purpose

Using AI to identify the exact gap between where you stand and where a target role expects you to be, then building a focused study plan that closes it without busywork.

03

Job Searching With a Clear System

Prompt patterns for filtering listings, decoding job descriptions, surfacing hidden requirements, and tracking applications in a way that does not consume an entire week.

04

Authentic Application Materials

Writing CVs, cover letters, LinkedIn summaries, and outreach emails with AI support while keeping your voice, your story, and your honest experience at the center of every line.

05

Tailoring at Scale Without Sounding Generic

Workflows that adapt the same application to many roles in minutes, without producing the bland, recycled tone that recruiters now recognize within a single paragraph.

06

Interview Practice With AI as Your Coach

Running mock interviews, behavioural question drills, technical walk throughs, and post interview debriefs with feedback on clarity, structure, confidence, and pacing.

07

Salary, Offer, and Negotiation Preparation

Prompts that help you research compensation ranges, prepare counter offers, and rehearse difficult conversations before they happen, so you arrive at the table already prepared.

08

Portfolio, Projects, and Proof of Work

How to use AI to plan, structure, document, and present the kind of side projects that make applications stand out, while keeping the credit for the work clearly with you.

09

A Strategic Personal Career Document

A one page personal career plan, refreshed each semester, that ties your studies, projects, and applications into one coherent direction instead of a scattered checklist.

10

Session Practical: Apply, Interview, Iterate

Participants tailor a real CV and cover letter for a chosen listing, then run a live mock interview round and walk out with concrete feedback to act on within the week.

Day 04
Session Four  ·  90 Minutes
04

Become the One Percent AI User

The closing session pulls together the tactics that quietly separate top operators from everyone else: platform awareness, context discipline, free tier mastery, and the pro features hidden in plain sight. Participants leave with a personal collaboration policy and a long term growth framework.

01

Platform Awareness

A clear eyed comparison of the major chat platforms, image tools, coding assistants, voice models, and research engines, so you stop reaching for one hammer on every job.

02

The Context Economy

A working definition of context windows, tokens, and memory budgets, and why every serious user thinks about context the way a careful spender thinks about money.

03

The Commandments of Context

A short, practical set of rules covering what to load first, what to hold back, when to summarise, when to start fresh, and how to keep long conversations sharp rather than slow.

04

Free Tier Maximisation

A practical playbook for getting near paid quality out of free accounts, including model selection, prompt routing, session hygiene, and the small habits that stretch every message further.

05

Stretching the Limit Without Hitting It

Techniques for splitting tasks, reusing outputs, batching questions, and holding reserve capacity so your free quota lasts a full study week rather than one productive afternoon.

06

The Pro Capabilities Most People Miss

A guided tour of features hiding in plain sight, including projects, custom instructions, file uploads, canvas modes, deep research tools, voice modes, and structured outputs.

07

Choosing the Right Tool for the Job

A decision guide that matches common tasks, such as writing, coding, research, slides, images, and data, to the platform that actually does the job best on a given day.

08

A Personal AI Collaboration Policy

A one page document that states, in your own words, where you welcome AI help, where you do the work alone, and how you disclose collaboration to teachers, teammates, and employers.

09

A Framework for Continued Growth

A quarterly self review structure that keeps your AI skills compounding long after the workshop ends, including how to track new releases, retire old habits, and grow without chasing every update.

10

Closing Practical: Your Personal Operating System

Each participant assembles a one page personal AI operating system: preferred platforms, context rules, free tier playbook, and collaboration policy, signed in their own name.
