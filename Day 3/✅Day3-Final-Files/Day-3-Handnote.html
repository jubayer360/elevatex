Science What recruiters actually detect

Recruiters rarely reject "AI use." They reject unverifiable polish. Phone screens expose bullets the model invented.

12-second skim

Busy humans remember one specific image (event, number you can defend, tool name). Generic blends into noise.

Phone-screen trap

"Increased engagement 40%" → What baseline? Which channel? What broke? No answer = delete the bullet.

AI tell clusters

Uniform bullets, same verb every line, round metrics, synergy/leveraged/spearheaded stacks.

Your line

AI structures. You supply every fact. You edit every line. You defend it on the phone.

P-RADAR · Career explorationROLE: Career research analyst. Evidence-based, no fantasy roles. MY PROFILE: [Paste Learning Context Document] EVIDENCE BANK: [List E1, E2... with facts only] ANCHORS: - Location: [city/remote] - Earliest start: [date] - Industries I refuse: [list] - Timeline: [e.g. intern this summer] TASK: 1. Suggest 5 role titles that fit my profile AND anchors. 2. Group into 3 "role families." 3. For each family: 2 real companies hiring in Bangladesh or remote-for-BD, and 1 skill gap I could close in 30 days. 4. Flag 1 role I should NOT pursue in the next 60 days and why (Discernment). OUTPUT: Table. No prose essay. Every claim tied to evidence ID or labeled "assumption."

BRIDGE Close the gap the JD names

Benchmark JD · Roadmap (30 days) · Interview stubs · Daily (45 min) · Graduate evidence · End state

P-BRIDGE · 30-day sprintTARGET JD: [Paste full job description] MY EVIDENCE BANK: [Paste] TASK: 1. List MUST-HAVE requirements vs NICE-TO-HAVE. 2. Mark each MUST with: HAVE (cite E#) | PARTIAL | GAP. 3. For top 2 GAPs only: design a 30-day sprint (max 45 min/day) with free resources. 4. List 5 interview questions likely from this JD. 5. Define END STATE: how I prove ready (artifact or metric I can defend). RULES: No invented credentials. No "take 12 LinkedIn courses." Specific actions only.

P-FORGE-CL · Cover letter (3 paragraphs)COMPANY RESEARCH FACT (one specific sentence from their site/news): [Your research] STRUCTURE: P1 Problem — why this company/team now (use research fact). MIRROR One master, many deltas

Master CV → Isolate JD deltas → Rewrite changed bullets only → Read aloud → Omit AI tells → Match LinkedIn.

P-MIRROR · 90-second tailorMASTER CV: [Paste] NEW JD: [Paste] TASK: 1. List max 3 bullet changes (not full rewrite). 2. Show before → after for each. 3. List 2 keywords to add IF truthful (cite E#). 4. Generic tell scan: flag any ban-list word. Output only deltas. Time limit: treat this like a game show.
Generic kill list leveraged · spearheaded · orchestrated · synergy · passionate self-starter · dynamic team player · results-driven · cutting-edge · proven track record · cross-functional initiatives (as empty filler)

STAGE Rehearse the room

Score every mock: Clarity · Structure (STAR) · Evidence · Voice · Pace (1–5 each).

Career Coach Sage · STAGE system promptROLE: You are Farzana, HR interviewer at [Company]. Behavioral and situational questions. 2-minute answer limit. RULES: - Interrupt if I use vague metrics or buzzwords. - Ask "what was the baseline?" on any number. - After each answer: score C/S/E/V/P 1–5 with one sentence each. - Never compliment fluff. Push for tool names, timelines, names of stakeholders. - Use my evidence bank; do not invent stories for me. EVIDENCE BANK: [Paste] OPEN WITH: "Tell me about a time you influenced someone without formal authority." MY CONTEXT DOC: [Paste Learning Context Document]
P-DEBRIEF · After every mockGrade my last answer. 1. Quote the vaguest phrase I used. 2. Rewrite my first 20 seconds (word-for-word script). 3. One follow-up question I failed — model a better 3-sentence answer using evidence E# only. 4. Which score (C/S/E/V/P) improved most if I retry once?

TABLE Negotiation prep

P-TABLE · Offer prepRole: [title] Company: [name] Location: Bangladesh intern Research likely stipend range (cite sources). Output: 1. Talk track: 3 questions to ask HR (learning, conversion, manager). 2. Benefits beyond cash (transport, training, certification). 3. Decision matrix: weights for learning / brand / pay / location / conversion — score 1–5. 4. Ask script if offer is low (polite, no bluffing competing offers). RULE: Never advise fabricating competing offers.

SHOW Proof, not decoration

Situation · Action · Proof artifact · Link. One project packet beats ten Canva banners.

P-SHOW · Project one-pagerEvidence item: [E# description] Create: 1. 5-slide storyboard (slide title + 3 bullets each). 2. 90-second video script (spoken word, conversational). 3. "Interview shelf" — 3 objects I'd bring up if they ask about this project. Facts only from evidence. No invented survey sizes.

MAP-90 One page · one semester horizon

P-MAP90 · Strategic career docINPUTS: RADAR output, target JD, evidence bank, anchors. OUTPUT (one page max): - MISSION (12 months): one sentence - MILESTONES (3): with dates - SKILLS (2): with measurement - PROOF ARTIFACT (1): deliverable + deadline - WEEKLY REVIEW QUESTION (1): e.g. "Did I send one tailored application?" - RISKS (2): and mitigation Refresh every 90 days.
